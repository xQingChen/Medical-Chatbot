All About Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

What is Conjunctivitis?

Conjunctivitis, commonly known as pink eye, is an inflammation or infection of the conjunctiva, the transparent membrane that lines your eyelid and covers the white part of your eyeball. When the small blood vessels in the conjunctiva become inflamed, they become more visible, causing the whites of your eyes to appear reddish or pink. Conjunctivitis can be highly contagious and is common among children, though it can affect people of all ages.

Causes, Symptoms, Treatment, and Prevention of Conjunctivitis

  1. Causes:
    • Viral Conjunctivitis: Caused by viruses such as adenoviruses or the same viruses that cause the common cold.
    • Bacterial Conjunctivitis: Caused by bacteria such as Staphylococcus aureus, Streptococcus pneumoniae, or Haemophilus influenzae.
    • Allergic Conjunctivitis: Triggered by allergens such as pollen, dust mites, pet dander, or mold.
    • Chemical Conjunctivitis: Caused by exposure to irritants like chlorine in swimming pools, smoke, or fumes.
  1. Symptoms:
    • Redness: The whites of the eyes appear pink or red.
    • Itching: A sensation of itching in one or both eyes, particularly in allergic conjunctivitis.
    • Tearing: Excessive tear production in the affected eye(s).
    • Discharge: Watery or thick discharge, depending on the cause (viral, bacterial).
    • Swelling: Swelling of the eyelids or conjunctiva.
    • Burning Sensation: A feeling of burning or irritation in the eyes.
    • Sensitivity to Light: Increased sensitivity to bright light.
  1. Treatment:
    • Viral Conjunctivitis: Typically no specific treatment; it usually clears up on its own. Symptom relief includes warm compresses and artificial tears.
    • Bacterial Conjunctivitis: Treated with antibiotic eye drops or ointments prescribed by a doctor.
    • Allergic Conjunctivitis: Treated with antihistamine eye drops and avoiding allergens.
    • Chemical Conjunctivitis: Rinse the eyes immediately with plenty of water and avoid further exposure to the irritant.
  1. Prevention:
    • Practice Good Hygiene: Wash your hands frequently with soap and water, especially before touching your face or eyes.
    • Avoid Sharing Personal Items: Do not share towels, pillowcases, or makeup with others.
    • Manage Allergies: Reduce exposure to allergens and use antihistamines as needed.
    • Disinfect Surfaces: Regularly clean and disinfect surfaces that are frequently touched.
    • Protect Your Eyes: Use goggles in swimming pools and avoid exposure to smoke or fumes.
    • Stay Home When Contagious: If you have viral or bacterial conjunctivitis, stay home until the infection clears.
